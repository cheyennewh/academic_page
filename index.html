About Me

Understanding the mind through neural computation

I investigate the intricate neural mechanisms underlying human cognition, focusing on how networks of neurons give rise to complex behaviors like memory formation, attention regulation, and decision-making processes.

My approach combines advanced neuroimaging techniques with computational modeling to understand how information flows through neural circuits and how these patterns change with learning, aging, and neurological conditions.

I believe that understanding the brain's computational principles will not only advance our knowledge of human cognition but also inform the development of more effective treatments for neurological and psychiatric disorders.

Research Projects

Active investigations into the neural basis of cognition

Neural Network Dynamics in Working Memory

2023 - Present | Principal Investigator

Attention Networks Across the Lifespan

2022 - 2024 | Co-Investigator

Longitudinal study tracking changes in attention network connectivity and efficiency from young adulthood through aging.

Synaptic Plasticity in Learning

2021 - 2023 | Research Fellow

Investigating how synaptic connections strengthen and reorganize during skill acquisition and memory consolidation.
