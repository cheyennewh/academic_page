About

I'm a cognitive scientist interested in why we remember what we do. My research focuses on understanding why certain things stick in our memory while others fade and how these processes unfold in the brain. Over eight years in memory research, I've developed expertise in large-scale web studies and in-lab experimentation (tDCS, eye-tracking). Much of my current work involves collecting large-sample behavioral data, which I wrangle with Python, R, and MATLAB using advanced statistical and modeling techniques to make sense of these complex patterns in human memory.
